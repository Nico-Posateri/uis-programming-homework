Van Jacobson and TCP/IP

Van Jacobson's Primary Contributions

Aside from Van Jacobson's seminal developments on TCP/IP which reduced internet congestion in time for the major traffic surge of the late 1980's, Jacobson has co-written a variety of tools that are equally relevant today.

This includes network diagnostic tools such as tcpdump, traceroute, and pathchar; as well as the Internet Multicast Backbone (MBone). He also laid the foundation for Voice over Internet Protocol (VoIP) applications with audio and video conferencing tools vic, vat, and wb.

Jacobson's algorithm for congestion wasn't just a method for speeding up Internet use for existing users, however. Firstly, this compression algorithm made the Internet more accessible for users with lower-end hardware and connections. Perhaps more important, though, is the lasting impact of this implementation.
